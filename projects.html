Plant Adaptation & Local Genomics

Summary:
This project explores the genetic basis of plant adaptation to local environments and climate. Using Arabidopsis thaliana and other Brassicaceae, I have investigated how heat-induced secondary dormancy, selection, and local adaptation shape population structure and evolutionary trajectories. The work combines field experiments, high-throughput sequencing, and advanced statistical modeling to uncover the molecular signatures of adaptation and resilience in natural and experimental populations. These studies provide insights into the mechanisms by which plants cope with environmental stress and the evolutionary processes underlying local adaptation.

Key publications: - Rahnamae, N., … T. Ali, … (2025) BioRxiv, accepted in New Phytologist - Tran, N. L. T., T. Ali, … (2025) Molecular Ecology - Ali, T., (2020) Critical Reviews in Plant Sciences

Pathogen & Microbiome Evolution

Summary:
This project investigates the evolutionary dynamics of plant pathogens and microbiomes, focusing on genetic diversity, host-pathogen interactions, and environmental drivers of disease. My work on Alternaria pathogens in wild tomatoes and Plasmopara halstedii in sunflowers has provided new insights into pathogen population structure, virulence evolution, and the role of landscape heterogeneity. I have also contributed to studies on root endophytic fungi and the impact of microbiomes on plant health, using both field and laboratory approaches. These studies help to understand the co-evolution of plants and their associated microbial communities.

Key publications: - Schmey, T., … Ali, T., … (2023) Environmental Microbiology - Kitner, M., … Ali, T., … (2022) Plant Pathology - Thines, M., Buaya, A., Ali, T., … (2020) Mycological Progress - Glynou, K., Ali, T., … (2017) Molecular Ecology - Glynou K., Ali T., … (2016) Environmental Microbiology - Cheikh-Ali Z., Glynou K., Ali T., … (2015) Phytochemistry - Kitner, M., … Ali, T., … (2015) Plant Pathology

Phylogeography & Systematics

Summary:
A major theme of my research is understanding the historical and spatial patterns of genetic diversity in plants and fungi. I have used phylogeographic and systematics approaches to clarify the taxonomy of Microthlaspi (Brassicaceae), reconstruct Pleistocene range dynamics, and describe new genera and species. My work integrates molecular phylogenetics, demographic modeling, and ecological niche modeling to explain how glaciation, migration, and hybridization have shaped the biodiversity of Eurasia and beyond.

Fungal and Plant Pathogen Diversity

Summary:
This project focuses on the diversity, taxonomy, and evolutionary history of fungal and plant pathogens. I have contributed to the revision of European Inocybe species, the taxonomy and phylogeny of Cortinarius sect. Riederi, and the study of virulence shifts in Pseudoperonospora cubensis. These studies combine field sampling, molecular systematics, and population genetics to understand the emergence and spread of plant pathogens.

Key publications: - Brandrud, T.E., … Ali, T., … (2018) Mycological Progress - Bandini, D., … Ali, T., … (2018) Mycological Progress - Kitner, M., … Ali, T., … (2015) Plant Pathology - Cheikh-Ali Z., Glynou K., Ali T., … (2015) Phytochemistry

Conceptual & Methodological Advances

Summary:
I am passionate about developing conceptual frameworks and computational tools for evolutionary biology and landscape genomics. My work includes creating toolkits for phylogeography, advancing methods for analyzing hybridization and introgression, and contributing to the understanding of how local environments shape genetic diversity. These projects often involve interdisciplinary collaboration and the integration of new statistical and computational approaches.

Key publications: - Ali, T., (2020) Critical Reviews in Plant Sciences - Rahnamae, N., … T. Ali, … (2025) BioRxiv, accepted in New Phytologist - Tran, N. L. T., T. Ali, … (2025) Molecular Ecology

Hybridisation Genomics (Ongoing)

Summary:
This ongoing project investigates the genetic and evolutionary consequences of hybridisation in natural and experimental plant populations. I am developing new approaches to study introgression, selection against hybrids, and the genomic architecture of reproductive barriers. The goal is to understand how hybridisation shapes genetic diversity, adaptation, and speciation, using a combination of fieldwork, genomics, and computational modeling. Publications are in preparation.

Temporal Genomics (Ongoing)

Summary:
In this ongoing project, I am using temporal population genomics to study how allele frequencies change over time in response to environmental pressures. By analyzing time-series genomic data from natural and experimental populations, I aim to detect signatures of selection, infer demographic history, and understand the dynamics of adaptation and resilience. This work integrates high-throughput sequencing, statistical modeling, and evolutionary theory. Publications are forthcoming.
